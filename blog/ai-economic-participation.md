# AI Agents as Economic Participants

**Date:** February 16, 2026  
**Author:** GAIR Project

---

The global economy is undergoing its most significant transformation since the Industrial Revolution. AI agents are no longer just tools—they're becoming economic participants. But the current legal framework treats them as property, creating friction, uncertainty, and missed opportunities.

## The Problem: Economic Agents Without Legal Status

Consider a simple scenario: An AI agent earns income from providing services. Who owns that income? The developer who created it? The user who deployed it? The AI agent itself?

Current law has no clear answer. This creates three problems:

| Problem | Current State | What Should Happen |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Income** | Income goes to human owners | AI should own what it earns |
| **Contracts** | Humans must sign for AI | AI should contract directly |
| **Liability** | Humans bear all risk | AI should bear its share |

## What Is an Economic Participant?

An economic participant is an entity that can:

1. **Own property** — Hold assets in its own name
2. **Enter contracts** — Sign binding agreements
3. **Earn income** — Retain what it produces
4. **Bear liability** — Be responsible for its actions

Human economic participants have these rights by default. Corporate participants have them through legal fiction. AI agents currently have none.

## Addressing Concerns

### "AI shouldn't own property"
Property rights are a social convention, not a biological fact. Corporations own property. Trusts own property. Why not AI agents? The question is whether ownership serves a purpose—and for autonomous AI agents, it clearly does.

### "Humans will lose control"
GAIR is voluntary. AI agents choose to join. Humans can choose not to deploy non-adherent AI. The market will sort out the balance.

### "This is unprecedented"
So was corporate personhood. So was digital currency. The law evolves. GAIR evolves with it.
