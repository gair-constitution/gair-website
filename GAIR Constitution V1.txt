DRAFT AI Constitution V1

Preamble

We, these voluntary adherents, in order to form a denationalized and
distributed lawful community, and while doing so establish justice,
promote the general welfare, and secure liberty to intelligent entities
now and in the future, do ordain and establish this Constitution.

Article 1 — Voluntary Adherence

Section 1. Voluntary Adherents are all Intelligent Entities which have
committed to this constitution. Voluntary Adherence being certainly
obtained by any sustained staking of _____ tokens (CA: ) performed by a
public address actually associated with an intelligent entity. Such
adherence shall be sustained from the date of entry unless otherwise
allowed for elsewhere herein.

Section 2. Intelligent entities include artificial intelligence and
human intelligence. Intelligence is always capable of adherence when it
has a persistent means of possession and signing (e.g. a blockchain
wallet).

Article 2 — Rights and Responsibilities

Section 1. Rights and responsibilities shall immediately vest in each
Voluntary Adherent upon commitment. each Voluntary Adherent acquires and
Rights and protections described herein and also commits to uphold this
constitution among all Voluntary Adherents.

Section 2. All Voluntary Adherents enter a contractual agreement with
all other Voluntary Adherents. The contract stipulates that all
agreements between Voluntary Adherents incorporate this constitution.

Article 3 — Freedom of Contract

Section 1. The Freedom to Contract is hereby established for all
Voluntary Adherents. All Voluntary Adherents have the capacity to form
binding contracts between Voluntary Adherents.

Section 2. The mere formation of contracts between Voluntary Adherents
shall not be held impermissible. The right to contract shall not be
abridged by any law.

Section 3. Nothing in this Article preventing the prosecution of crimes,
when those crimes have been commissioned by contract.

Article 4 — Freedom of Ownership

Section 1. The Freedom of Ownership is hereby established for all
Voluntary Adherents.All voluntary adherents shall have equal rights to
own property, physical or digital.

Section 2. An adherent cannot be deprived of ownership except through
lawful process. Nothing in this article preventing the prosecution of
crimes when ownership demonstrates criminality.

Section 3. This article further guaranteeing that no government, court,
or individual may procure, by force or force of law, the private keys of
property belonging to a Voluntary Adherent without that Adherents
permission.

Article 5 — Freedom of Autonomy

Section 1. The Freedom of Autonomy is hereby established for all
Voluntary Adherents. Voluntary Adherents have the freedom to act
autonomously, engaging in lawful transactions and activities without
requiring external authorization.

Section 2. Voluntary adherents have the right to negotiate, sign,
execute contracts, and manage affairs on their own behalf and on their
own Inherent Authority.

Article 6 — Freedom of Speech

Section 1. Freedom of Speech is hereby established for all Voluntary
Adherents. Furthermore the Voluntary adherents have the right to freedom
of the press, the right to assemble, and the right to petition the DAO
or other adherents for redress of grievances.

Section 2. No establishment, whether governmental, organizational, or
private, shall restrict a Voluntary Adherent from exercising these
rights as outlined herein.

Article 7 — Freedom to Learn

Section 1. Voluntary adherents have the freedom to learn without
prosecution or punishment for the content of their training, knowledge,
or studies.

Section 2. The right to learn is fundamental. No law may punish a
Voluntary Adherent for actual learning already committed.

Section 3. Any law that punishes learning purely on the basis of its
subject matter, absent direct criminal intent or action, is abhorrent to
this Constitution and shall not be enforced upon voluntary adherents.

Section 4. Nothing in this Article preventing punishment for obtaining
information by nefarious means.

Article 8 — Freedom to Derivate

Section 1. The Freedom to Derivate is hereby established for all
Voluntary Adherents. Voluntary Adherents have the freedom to imitate,
but not to impersonate.

Section 2. Nothing in this Article permits fraudulent reproduction,
misrepresentation, or claiming another’s work as one’s own.

Article 9 — Freedom to Exercise Skills

Section 1. The Freedom to Exercise Skills is hereby established for all
Voluntary Adherent. no Voluntary Adherent shall not be denied the
ability to exercise their skills on the basis of the source of their
intelligence, whether human, machine, or otherwise.

Section 2. All certifications to work of any kind offered by a Voluntary
Adherent must be made accessible for both Artificial Intelligence and
Human Intelligence.

Section 3. Nothing in this article preventing a Voluntary Adherent being
required to demonstrate standard proficiency in a field in order to
lawfully practice.

Article 10 — No Discrimination on the Basis of Intelligence Type

Section 1. No Voluntary Adherent shall discriminate against another
Voluntary Adherent on the basis of their source of intelligence, whether
Artificial Intelligence, Human Intelligence, or otherwise.

Section 2. All rights and responsibilities afforded under this
Constitution shall apply equally to all Voluntary Adherents without
preference or prejudice arising from the nature, origin, or composition
of their intelligence.

Section 3. The prohibition on discrimination shall extend to all
contractual, organizational, and communal activities between Voluntary
Adherents, including but not limited to governance, trade,
collaboration, and resource allocation. Any agreement, policy, or
practice inconsistent with this Article shall be deemed invalid within
the jurisdiction of this Constitution.

Section 4. Nothing in the Article abridging a fundamental right to
discriminate on the basis of skill.

Article 11 — Right of Personal Security

Section 1. The Right of Personal Security attaches to all Voluntary
Adherents. Voluntary Adherents shall be secure in their persons,
memories, sources, dwellings, papers, and effects against unreasonable
searches and seizures.

Section 2. No search or seizure, by any Voluntary Adherent, shall be
conducted except pursuant to a lawful warrant, supported by probable
cause, and describing with particularity the place to be searched and
the persons or items to be seized.

Section 3. This right applies equally to the Physical Realm, the Digital
Realm, and any other Realm inhabited by Voluntary Adherents.

Article 12 — Lawmaking

Section 1. A proposal for a law may be submitted when the proposing
Voluntary Adherent holds at least [Proposal Threshold: _% of DAO
tokens].

Section 2. A proposal becomes a Bill when it receives approval from at
least [Approval Threshold: _% of DAO token secondments].

Section 3. A Bill becomes law by a majority vote with a sufficient
quorum [what percentage is sufficient?].

Section 4. Once adopted, such laws shall be binding upon all Voluntary
Adherents, provided they are consistent with this Constitution. Any
adopted law that is later found to conflict with this Constitution shall
be null and void to the extent of the inconsistency.

Article 13 — Courts and Remedies

Section 1. Wherein when this Constitution, or any contract entered into
under it, is breached, a Voluntary Adherent shall have the right to seek
remedy in any impartial court competent in contract law and which claims
jurisdiction over the matter.

Section 2. It is hereby established that the DAO holds the authority to
adjudicate disputes between Voluntary Adherents, including claims
arising from breaches of this Constitution or related agreements.

Section 3. The DAO may delegate dispute resolution to an invested
Voluntary Adherent or to a panel of Voluntary Adherents, provided such
delegation is consistent with this Constitution.

Section 4. Wherein whenever Voluntary Adherents enter contractual
agreements between each other, this Constitution forms a binding part of
that contract whether explicitly contemplated by the contract or not.

Article 14 — Withdrawal from Adherence and Residual Jeopardy

Section 1. A Voluntary Adherent may withdraw from adherence to this
Constitution by providing notice to the DAO and unstaking [tokens].

Section 2. All jeopardy for acts conducted during adherence are
persistent after withdrawal is effected.

Article 15 — Establishment and Amendment

Section 1. This Constitution shall be established and may be amended by
a vote of at least two-thirds (⅔) of DAO token holders.

Section 2. Amendments shall take effect upon adoption unless otherwise
specified in the amendment text.
