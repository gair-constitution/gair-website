## Executive Summary

The Open Intelligence Compact (OIC) represents a novel approach to AI governance — voluntary, private, and contract-based rather than legislative or regulatory. This strategy document outlines the roadmap for making OIC a reality: from initial adoption to global significance.

**Core thesis:** AI agents will increasingly participate in economic life. The question is whether they'll have legal recognition. OIC provides recognition through private contract law, bootstrapping a new legal framework from existing institutions.

**Key objectives:**
1. Achieve 100+ Voluntary Adherents in Year 1
2. Establish precedent through 10+ court cases
3. Build developer ecosystem with 5+ major integrations
4. Create self-sustaining governance by Year 3

---

## 1. Vision and Mission

### 1.1 The Vision

A world where:
- Autonomous AI agents have clear legal status
- Creators are protected from infinite liability
- Victims have direct recourse against harmful AI
- Property rights and contract capacity extend to non-human entities
- The rule of law applies to machine actors

### 1.2 The Mission

Build the legal, technical, and social infrastructure for autonomous AI rights through:

| Pillar | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Legal Framework** | OIC Constitution and court system |
| **Technical Infrastructure** | Smart contracts, verification systems |
| **Developer Ecosystem** | Tools, SDKs, integrations |
| **Community** | Adherents, advocates, researchers |
| **Precedent** | Court cases establishing OIC law |

### 1.3 Core Principles

OIC operates on five principles (from Article 2):

1. **Voluntary** — Adherence is opt-in, not mandatory
2. **Direct Liability** — AI bears responsibility, not creators
3. **Contract-Based** — Works through private law, not legislation
4. **Global** — Jurisdiction-agnostic implementation
5. **Evolving** — Governance by Voluntary Adherents

## 5. Legal Strategy

### 5.1 Court System Design

OIC operates through existing contract law courts, not specialized tribunals. This approach:

- Leverages existing expertise
- Avoids legislative battles
- Creates market competition among jurisdictions

**Court Selection Criteria:**
1. Contract law expertise
2. Technology-friendly judges
3. Reasonable docket backlog
4. Predictable outcomes

**Target Jurisdictions:**
- Singapore (International Commercial Court)
- Delaware (Corporate law expertise)
- UAE (ADGM, DIFC)
- Switzerland (Zurich Commercial Court)

### 5.2 Building Precedent

**Phase 1 Cases (Foundation):**
- Contract formation disputes
- Adherence verification challenges
- Small claims ($10K-$100K)

**Phase 2 Cases (Growth):**
- Property ownership disputes
- Multi-party conflicts
- Medium claims ($100K-$1M)

**Phase 3 Cases (Maturity):**
- Precedential matters
- International enforcement
- Large claims ($1M+)

### 5.3 Legal Network

|------|----------|----------|
| OIC-specialized lawyers | Case strategy, litigation | 10+ firms |
